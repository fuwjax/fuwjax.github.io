The problem isn't that I don't like women, it's that I don't like people. To be fair that's not even the problem; I just really don't like emotions, I don't trust desires, and I couldn't care less about warm fuzzies.

A friend of mine told me recently that she used to want to date me. The underlying implication of course is that I screwed that up somehow, which really only serves to show that I don't particularly care.

Another friend of mine told me that our relationship was getting awkward because she wasn't sure what I wanted. I've realized two things from that conversation. The first is that I absolutely adore this woman; her smile, her laugh, her conversation, her touch are treasures to be guarded and protected with every ounce of strength in me. The second is that I don't particularly care.

Now don't get me wrong. I care about these women deeply. I just don't particularly care about their emotions or desires or making sure their fuzzy stays warm. At least, I don't particularly care about their emotions or desires with respect to me.

I am perfectly content doing my part to make these women feel all warm and fuzzy when they're around. Truth is, sometimes I think that's really the only reason women let us anywhere near them, just to balance their emotions, fulfill their desires, and leave them slightly warmer and a little fuzzier.

But I have to admit, I don't trust people who want to do the same for me.
